# Which Voron should I build?

> There's no wrong choice — there's the right choice **for you**. This page guides you based on your priorities.

---

## The 4 official models

| Model | Bed size | Z kinematics | Estimated kit price | Best for |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| **V0.2** | 120×120mm | 2 screws (manual) | ~$350–500 | Compact, lower budget, technical challenge |
| **Trident** | 250/300/350mm | 3 motors (auto) | ~$800–1400 | Versatile, reliable, recommended for first builds |
| **V2.4** | 250/300/350mm | 4 motors (auto) | ~$1000–1800 | Maximum performance, high-temp materials |
| **Switchwire** | 250×210mm | Moving bed (Prusa-style) | ~$600–900 | Ender 3 conversion, familiar format |

---

## By priority

### 💶 Limited budget → **V0.2**
The cheapest kit, the smallest machine. Ideal for discovering Klipper and the Voron community without a heavy investment. Z leveling is manual (3 screws) — no automatic QGL.

> ⚠️ The V0 is mechanically more demanding to assemble than the Trident. "Budget beginner" doesn't mean "technical beginner".

---

### 🏆 First Voron, general use → **Trident**
The Trident is the community's choice for a serious first build. 3 Z motors for reliable automatic leveling, large bed available in 250/300/350mm, excellent rigidity. Less complex than the V2.4.

**The default recommended choice.**

---

### 🔥 High temperature, high performance → **V2.4**
4 Z motors for the best leveling, chamber that reaches 60°C+ easily, AWD-compatible. The V2.4 is the "no compromise" machine of the lineup.

> The V2.4 requires more sourcing and tuning than the Trident. For builders who want to push limits.

---

### ♻️ Repurpose an Ender 3 / Creality → **Switchwire**
The Switchwire reuses an Ender 3 frame. If you have a Creality lying around and want to transform it into a serious Klipper machine, this is the path.

---

## By bed size

| Need | Recommended bed size |
|--------|----------------------|
| Figurines, small parts | 250mm |
| General use | 300mm ← most common |
| Large parts (panels, enclosures) | 350mm |

> 300mm is the community sweet spot. More mods available, more experience reports, and chamber heating is easier than with 350mm.

---

## By target material

| Filament | Recommended model |
|----------|------------------|
| PLA / PETG only | V0.2 or Trident |
| Regular ABS / ASA | Trident (closed enclosure sufficient) |
| High-speed ABS, PA, PC | V2.4 (60°C+ chamber, maximum rigidity) |
| Flexibles (TPU) | Any model with a good extruder |

---

## TL;DR

> **Don't know what to choose → get a Trident 300mm.** You won't regret it.
